THE PROBLEM

Parkinson's doesn't keep a schedule,
So why does the pill bottle?

Medication works until it doesn't. As the day goes on, on time gets shorter and off periods, stiffness, slowness, tremor, arrive earlier than the clock predicts. Fixed dosing times can't keep up with a body that shifts hour to hour.

SYMPTOM ONE · TIMING

off episodes

the dose wears off before the next one is due and symptoms return, stiffness, slowness, tremor. absorption shifts with meals and gut speed, so the same pill at the same time lands differently from one day to the next. most people only find out they're off once they're already in it, and the next dose can take up to an hour to work.

SYMPTOM TWO · MOVEMENT

freezing of gait

the feet suddenly feel glued to the floor while the body keeps leaning forward. it strikes in doorways, on turns, and while crossing the street, a leading cause of falls and a big reason people stop leaving the house. today's cueing tools only switch on after the freeze has already started.

both problems take the same thing away: the ability to trust your own body in public.

WHO IT'S FOR

Built for the middle of the journey.

Cadence is made for people in the mid-to-later stages of Parkinson's, on levodopa, living with motor fluctuations, many of them older adults. they want to keep going out, keep their independence, and stop bracing for a fall, and they're tired of dosing blind. the people around them carry that worry too, and the neurologist who sets the schedule sees them for about fifteen minutes every few months.

TWO BANDS, ONE FORECAST

Two rhythms the body keeps, read at once.

Parkinson's plays out on two clocks at the same time, a chemical one and a mechanical one. Cadence puts a sensor on each and feeds both into a single model that forecasts your motor state through the day.

WRIST BAND · CHEMICAL RHYTHM

how your body answers the last dose

the wrist reads motor state plus the autonomic signals, heart rate, heart-rate variability, skin response and temperature, that shift before a person consciously feels an off period starting.

buzzes the dose nudge
ANKLE BAND · MECHANICAL RHYTHM

the gait that breaks down before a freeze

the ankle reads the gait pattern and the small breakdown in stride that shows up in the seconds before the feet freeze, the signal a wrist alone is too far away to catch.

pulses the freeze cue, before it lands

the bands are screenless on purpose, tremor and slowness make a small touchscreen hard to use. a magnetic clasp and one shared charging dock mean nothing fiddly for a shaky hand, and everything to read moves to the phone, where the text can be large.

THE MODELS

From raw motion to a forecast you can act on.

Raw signal · 200 Hz Feature windows State + forecast models On / off + dose nudge
State classifier

A temporal model labels each minute as on, off, or uncertain from movement features, the engine behind the live state.

Wearing-off forecaster

A sequence model projects your levodopa response forward to estimate the next likely off window as a calibrated range.

Personalization layer

A lightweight per-patient head adapts the base model to your own rhythm over the first weeks of wear.

Models are trained on de-identified, consented data and validated against clinician-rated motor state. Cadence supports clinical decisions, it does not make them.

HONESTLY, WHAT'S NEW

Detection is established. Acting on the forecast is the part no one has shipped.

There are two layers in Cadence, and only the top one is new. We're specific about that on purpose.

BASE LAYER · DETECTION

scoring symptoms minute to minute

calling the on/off state from sensor signals is established, well-trodden research. it's the foundation Cadence stands on, not the part we claim to have invented.

TOP LAYER · FORECASTING + ACTION

turning the forecast into a decision

predicting the next off window and the pre-freeze moment, then turning each into a just-in-time dose nudge and a preemptive cue, inside one screenless two-sensor product. that closed loop, to the patient, is the part no one has built. *this technology requires time to learn your personal signals, our product will not predict accurately out of the box but will improve greatly with time. the training will be shown as a progress bar in the patient, clinician, as well as caretaker apps. 

research has shown off episodes and freezes can be predicted to a degree, small studies have forecast wearing-off from wearable and autonomic signals, and pre-freeze prediction has reached the mid-eighties in sensitivity. cadence doesn't claim to invent prediction. our goal is to develop a personalized system that turns the forecast into an action, for the patient, instead of stopping at a report for the doctor.

OUR STORY

We started with a
simple purpose.


the inspiration behind cadence began during a trip to india, where a member of our team witnessed firsthand the daily toll parkinson's disease takes on a family. he watched his parnani, his maternal great-grandmother, and her caretakers lose precious time constantly stressing over medication schedules and struggling to keep her symptoms under control. seeing this endless cycle of guesswork sparked a mission: to build an intelligent, intuitive tool that handles the complex math of dosing, giving families back their peace of mind and time to focus on what truly matters. we built cadence to expand those vital windows of 'on-time,' empowering families to spend less energy managing symptoms and more time making beautiful memories during their best hours.
